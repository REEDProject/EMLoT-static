Viewing Event Record: Requests, Woodford vs Holland: The arbitration commissioner delivers his report

Abstract

Clement Goldsmith delivers the report he wrote with Anthony Dyot to the Court of Requests on the suit brought by Thomas Woodford against Aaron Holland concerning the lease and profits of the Red Bull. The report directs that Holland should renew Woodford's lease for one eighteenth part of the profits, maintaining the conditions of the lease made to Thomas Swinnerton and Philip Stone, who both had the lease in question before it was purchased by Woodford. The report awards the question of the gatherer's fee to Holland and that Woodford should repay Holland the gatherer's fees which he had been until then collecting. Furthermore, they order Woodford to pay Holland Â£6 13s 4p and the 25s owed for half a year's rent. Goldsmith ends by noting that Woodford has decided to return to court rather than abide by the arbitration.
