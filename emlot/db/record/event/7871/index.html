Viewing Event Record: Chancery, de Caine vs Wintershall: Ellis Worth deposes

Abstract

In a Chancery suit between Margaret Wintershall, daughter of Fortune sharer Richard Gunnell, and former player Andrew de Caine, former player Ellis Worth deposes that the motivation for bonds between players and sharers was to keep the companies together. Because it was 'vnlawfull to bynd players of Actors' to a specific playhouse, the bonds were instead expressed monetarily. Worth also deposes on the status of theatrical apprentices in London, stating that it was 'vsuall vsage & Custome' for established players to bind youths to themselves or to other freemen, and that these apprentices 'Did vsually Acte & play partes in Comidies and Tragidyes att the fortune & other play houses.' If these apprentices completed the appropriate period of service, Worth recalls that they 'were made free of such particuler Trades as their severall Masters vsed besides playing.'
